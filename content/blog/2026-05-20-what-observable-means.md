## The word got bought

Somewhere along the way, "observability" stopped being a property of a system and became a product category. Vendors sell it now. You can apparently buy observability the way you buy disk space.

I think that's backwards.

## My definition

A system is observable when you can ask it a question you didn't have in mind at deploy time and get an answer without shipping new code.

## What it is not

It is not three pillars. The pillars are ingredients, and having flour doesn't mean you have bread. It's also not a dashboard count. I've seen systems with fifty dashboards that nobody could debug.
